Genesis

Born on Bitcoin

On September 28, 2023, the call went out: “FREE TO INSCRIBE — FIRST COME FIRST SERVE — 3333 TOSHIS.” The Toshi Order was born — a collection of 3,333 unique inscriptions etched permanently into Bitcoin through Ordinals.

By October 4, the Order was forming. Each Toshi cost roughly $1.10 to inscribe at 10 sats/vb. The original minting site at thetoshiorder.github.io showed available Toshis, while OrdinalsBot handled hash checks and inscriptions on rare sats. UniSat was used to verify unconfirmed inscriptions. By October 5, the end of the mint was near — all 3,333 Toshis claimed, the Order formed and FINAL.

What began as an anonymous founder’s vision quickly evolved into something greater: a decentralized collective governed by The Fellowship — ten stewards chosen to carry the mission forward. No VCs. No roadmap. Just culture, conviction, and the immutable permanence of Bitcoin.

Governance

The Fellowship

In November 2023, the anonymous founder transferred stewardship of The Toshi Order to a council of ten — The Fellowship. They govern the project’s direction, community, and mission.

The Fellowship holds the keys to the official channels and carries the flame of the original vision: decentralized, community-first, forever on Bitcoin.
